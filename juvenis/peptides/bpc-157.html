Overview

What Is BPC-157?

BPC-157 is a 15-amino-acid peptide sequence derived from a protein naturally found in gastric juice. "Body Protective Compound" is exactly what the name implies — it protects and repairs tissue throughout the body. It has been the subject of extensive preclinical research, with results showing remarkable regenerative properties across multiple tissue types.

How It Works

BPC-157 Mechanisms of Action

BPC-157 doesn't work through a single pathway — it activates multiple healing cascades simultaneously, which is why it's effective across such a wide range of conditions.

Angiogenesis

Stimulates the formation of new blood vessels, improving blood supply to damaged tissue and accelerating the delivery of nutrients needed for repair.

Tendon Fibroblast Growth

Upregulates growth hormone receptors in tendon fibroblasts, dramatically increasing the rate of tendon and ligament repair.

Nitric Oxide Modulation

Modulates nitric oxide production to reduce inflammation while preserving healthy blood flow to healing tissues.

Growth Factor Upregulation

Increases expression of VEGF, EGF, and other growth factors that drive tissue regeneration across multiple organ systems.

Gut Barrier Repair

Directly promotes healing of intestinal epithelium, making it especially effective for gut permeability issues and GI inflammation.

Neuroprotection

Protects nerve cells from damage and supports peripheral nerve repair — beneficial after injury or surgery involving nerve tissue.

Frequently Asked Questions

BPC-157 — Common Questions

What is BPC-157?

BPC-157 (Body Protective Compound-157) is a synthetic peptide derived from a protein found in gastric juice. It has demonstrated the ability to accelerate healing of tendons, ligaments, muscles, and gut tissue across extensive preclinical research.

Is BPC-157 available via telehealth?

Yes. BPC-157 is a non-controlled peptide and can be prescribed via telehealth to patients in all 50 states. You don't need to be a Florida resident to receive a BPC-157 prescription from Juvenis Medical.

How is BPC-157 administered?

Most commonly via subcutaneous injection once or twice daily, ideally near the site of injury. For gut-specific conditions, oral capsules are also an option. We'll walk you through the injection process — it's simple and most patients become comfortable within one or two doses.

How long before I see results?

Many patients notice reduced pain and improved function within 2–4 weeks. More complete tissue repair typically occurs at 6–12 weeks, depending on the severity and chronicity of the injury.

Is BPC-157 safe?

BPC-157 has shown an excellent safety profile across preclinical studies, with no significant adverse effects identified. It is naturally occurring in human gastric juice in trace amounts. As with any peptide therapy, it should be supervised by a knowledgeable clinician.

What's the difference between BPC-157 and TB-500?

Both promote tissue healing, but via different mechanisms. BPC-157 works more locally and is especially strong for tendons, ligaments, and gut. TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4) acts more systemically and is strong for muscle repair. They are frequently combined for comprehensive recovery support.
